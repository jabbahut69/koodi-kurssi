An archway carves an entrance to the Sainsbury grounds. Outsiders are rarely able to catch a glimpse of the castle's true form due to the absurdely high walls surrounding it. You are one of the lucky ones, you think to yourself as you take a wary step inside.

Your invitation had arrived only a day before the unfortunate news of the sudden death of the young baron, the previous owner of the estate. Your appointment had, however, not been cancelled, so you decided to take the opportunity you had been awaiting for a long time. It seems a much worse idea now than when you confidently left your house that morning.

Could it be you are intruding the family in mourning? And such an important family at that.

You had not been able to sleep well the night before due to your excitement, so you had got up and made your way to the castle much too early. There is no one yet to receive you. You are too nervous to stay still, so you make way to the castle at your own accord. Fortunately, you had been able to acquire a map of the grounds from the university library. Where will you head first?

The Sainsburies have lived in the Sainsbury castle for generations. The castle was first built somewhere in the 16th century, but has been expanded and renovetad several times since.

The main entrance lies on the Northern side of tha castle. It leads to the Outer Court, which makes way to the more intimate spaces of the castle. You will find the old Sally Port to the West.
